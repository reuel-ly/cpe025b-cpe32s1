Penguins: Masters of the Antarctic Seas

About Penguins

Penguins are flightless seabirds that live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere. They are highly adapted for life in water, with streamlined bodies and strong flippers. Their black and white coloring provides camouflage while swimming.

Fun Fact

Male penguins often propose to their mates by presenting them with pebbles. If the female accepts the pebble, they become a pair, showcasing a charming courtship ritual.

More Penguin Facts

Excellent Swimmers

Penguins can swim up to 22 mph in water. Their flippers act like powerful paddles.

Deep Divers

Emperor penguins can dive deeper than 500 meters and stay underwater for more than 20 minutes.

Built for Cold

Penguins have thick blubber and dense feathers that protect them from freezing temperatures.
